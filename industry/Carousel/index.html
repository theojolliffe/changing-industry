Background on the UK Standard Industrial Classification

The UK SIC framework provides a way of classifying industrial activities into a standard hierarchal structure.

'Sections', which represent top level industries, are broken down into 'divisions' and then 'groups', classes' and 'subclasses'.

The UK SIC is based on the EU's NACE framework, but with the addition of a fifth level of the structural hierarchy.

It was first created in 1948 and has been revised multiple times to reflect the changing workforce in the UK. The most recent changes were made in 2007.
